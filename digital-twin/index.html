Digital Twin

A digital twin is a virtual representation of a physical object or system, created using data and analytics. It is used to simulate and analyze the characteristics, behavior, and performance of the corresponding physical object or system in order to understand and optimize its performance and predict future outcomes.

Digital twins can be used in a variety of applications, such as manufacturing, transportation, healthcare, and infrastructure. For example, a digital twin of a factory floor could be used to simulate and optimize the production process, or a digital twin of a bridge could be used to analyze and predict the performance of the bridge under different conditions.

To create a digital twin, data about the physical object or system is collected and used to create a virtual model. This model can then be used to simulate and analyze the behavior and performance of the physical object or system under various conditions and scenarios. The digital twin can be updated with new data as the physical object or system changes, allowing it to remain accurate and up-to-date.

Overall, digital twins are a powerful tool for understanding and optimizing the performance of complex physical systems and have the potential to revolutionize the way we design, build, and operate these systems.

 

Digital twin technology, with engineering data at its core, is helping engineers gain control over their assets. By connecting the right people to the right data and the right processes, engineers gain greater end-to-end insights. They can quickly identify the actions and strategies needed to deliver sustainable performance improvements

A digital twin combines the real world with the digital world. By capturing real-time data, the digital twin understands the current state, simulates the future state and is a basis for optimization. It enables to detect problems early, can be used as a basis for in-silico testing and is an opportunity to come to improved controls
